## **Meeting Minutes: Meeting with Bruce**

**Focus:** Project Validation, Industry Pain Points, and Pilot Integration

### **1\. Core Project Objectives**

- **Targeting the "First & Last Mile":** The project aims to secure the collection and drop\-off points to prevent "false pickups" \(unauthorized drivers/trucks\)\.
- **The "Four\-Key" Verification:** Validation depends on matching Driver ID, Vehicle ID, Shipment Location, and Final Destination\.
- **Blockchain Integration:** Using an immutable ledger to store these "handshakes" so that no admin or external actor can alter the record of who moved what, and when\.

### **2\. Key Industry Pain Points \(The "Real World" Perspective\)**

- **Asset vs\. Parcel Tracking:** Bruce emphasized that while tracking the 18\-meter truck \(the asset\) is common, there is a massive gap in tracking the **individual parcel** within that asset \(deconsolidation\)\.
- **Driver Identity Risk:** Hijackers often pose as legitimate drivers at warehouse gates\. Current systems \(access cards/taps\) are vulnerable to cloning\.
- **Internal Collusion:** Even with vetting, "disenfranchised" drivers may become complicit in thefts or "scoops\."
- **High Insurance Costs:** Logistics companies pay roughly **12\.5% of total cost per kg** toward insurance\. A more secure system directly lowers premiums and increases yield\.
- **The "Ad\-Hoc" Market:** While 30% of business is long\-term contracts \(e\.g\., FedEx\), 70% is ad\-hoc\. These customers need a "one\-click" secure solution without rigorous registration\.

### **3\. Technical Requirements & Observations**

- **Hardware/Connectivity:**

    - Bruce noted that ~11% of trucks go "off\-grid" or off\-route\.
    - The system must handle **offline verification** \(Bluetooth/NFC\) that syncs once signal is restored\.
    - Existing tech includes **Geofencing Locks** \(doors only unlock at the destination GPS coordinate\)\.

- **Smart Contracts:** Tom explained these will be used to define "rules of engagement" \(e\.g\., the five checks required before a contract is "passed"\)\.
- **API Integration:** The system needs to be able to "handshake" with existing platforms like *Parcel Perfect*, *IDVS*, and *Pulset*\.

### **4\. Strategic Advice from Bruce**

- **Don't Reinvent the Wheel:** Look at existing "Touch on Glass" \(mobile signature\) protocols and build on top of them rather than trying to replace them entirely\.
- **Focus on the Risk Portfolio:** If the system reduces the hijack rate from 10% to 2%, the insurance savings alone make the product viable\.
- **South African Context:** Logistics here is "archaic" and high\-risk; the niche is creating a "South African\-ready" security layer that global systems lack\.
